Traversing the martian surface

Inspired by a project I did a while ago.

Imagine the map below represents the surface of Mars. Different shades represent different altitudes. We are sending a rover to explore the surface, however, the rover has limited energy and hence will only explore regions of the same altitude as where it lands. Clicking on a cell indicates the landing location and the algorithm selected will then show the surrounding region that the rover will explore.

This visualization illustrates the different ways that Depth-First and Breadth-First Search traverse surrounding nodes.
